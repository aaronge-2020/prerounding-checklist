## 1. Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

Category: Diabetes and Blood Sugar Disorders
Quality status: pass
Current app support: registry gap - needs validated clinical intent/knowledge pack before app recommendations
Guidelines: ADA_SOC_2026; ADA_DIAGNOSIS_2026

Clinical questions:
- Polyuria, polydipsia, weight change, blurred vision, infections.
- ASCVD, CKD, HF, neuropathy, retinopathy, sleep apnea, steroid exposure.

Focused physical exam:
- BP, BMI/waist, volume if symptomatic.
- Foot skin/pulses/monofilament or vibration when neuropathy risk.
- Cardiovascular and thyroid exam when indicated.

Diagnostic workup and reference values:
- A1c, fasting plasma glucose, or 2-hour OGTT to confirm.
- CMP/eGFR, urine albumin-creatinine ratio, fasting lipids.
- Ketones/anion gap if vomiting, weight loss, dehydration, or acute symptoms.

Reference ranges / diagnostic thresholds:
- A1c: normal <5.7%; prediabetes 5.7-6.4%; diabetes >=6.5%.
- Fasting plasma glucose: normal <100 mg/dL; prediabetes 100-125; diabetes >=126.
- 2-hour 75-g OGTT: normal <140 mg/dL; prediabetes 140-199; diabetes >=200.
- Random plasma glucose >=200 mg/dL with classic symptoms supports diabetes diagnosis.
- UACR normal <30 mg/g; moderately increased 30-300; severely increased >300.

Red flags:
- DKA/HHS symptoms, dehydration, vomiting, altered mental status.
- Random glucose >=200 mg/dL with classic symptoms.

Results that change management:
- Diabetes-range A1c/FPG/OGTT changes diagnosis and treatment intensity.
- UACR >=30 or reduced eGFR changes kidney-protective therapy.

## 2. Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

Category: Diabetes and Blood Sugar Disorders
Quality status: pass
Current app support: registry gap - needs validated clinical intent/knowledge pack before app recommendations
Guidelines: ADA_SOC_2026; ADA_DIAGNOSIS_2026; ADA_HYPERGLYCEMIC_CRISES_2024

Clinical questions:
- Rapid polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss.
- Vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing, confusion.
- Personal/family autoimmune disease.

Focused physical exam:
- Vitals, hydration, mental status.
- Respiratory pattern/Kussmaul concern.
- Abdomen and infection/skin trigger exam.

Diagnostic workup and reference values:
- A1c/glucose criteria plus ketones/acid-base if symptomatic.
- Beta-hydroxybutyrate, BMP, venous/arterial pH, bicarbonate, anion gap.
- Islet autoantibodies: GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, insulin Ab if needed; C-peptide with concurrent glucose.

Reference ranges / diagnostic thresholds:
- A1c: normal <5.7%; prediabetes 5.7-6.4%; diabetes >=6.5%.
- Fasting plasma glucose: normal <100 mg/dL; prediabetes 100-125; diabetes >=126.
- 2-hour 75-g OGTT: normal <140 mg/dL; prediabetes 140-199; diabetes >=200.
- Random plasma glucose >=200 mg/dL with classic symptoms supports diabetes diagnosis.
- DKA: diabetes/hyperglycemia plus ketosis plus metabolic acidosis; glucose may be lower in euglycemic DKA.
- DKA beta-hydroxybutyrate often >=3.0 mmol/L with pH <7.3 or bicarbonate <18 mmol/L.

Red flags:
- Vomiting, dehydration, hypotension, Kussmaul respirations, altered mental status.
- SGLT2 inhibitor with ketotic symptoms even if glucose is not very high.

Results that change management:
- Ketosis plus acidosis requires urgent DKA pathway.
- Positive antibodies or low C-peptide supports autoimmune insulin-deficient diabetes.

- BP and weight.
- Volume status if vomiting/hyperglycemia.
- Pregnancy-focused assessment with OB team.

- Fasting plasma glucose: normal <100 mg/dL; prediabetes 100-125; diabetes >=126.
- 2-hour 75-g OGTT: normal <140 mg/dL; prediabetes 140-199; diabetes >=200.
- Random plasma glucose >=200 mg/dL with classic symptoms supports diabetes diagnosis.

Red flags:
- Ketones, vomiting/dehydration, severe hyperglycemia.
- Hypertension or preeclampsia features.

Results that change management:
- OGTT threshold crossing changes maternal/fetal monitoring and therapy.
- Postpartum abnormal OGTT changes long-term prevention.

## 6. Hypothyroidism (Underactive Thyroid)

Category: Thyroid Disorders
Quality status: pass
Current app support: routine_thyroid_disease_v1 (53)
Guidelines: ATA_HYPOTHYROIDISM_2014; AACE_ATA_HYPOTHYROIDISM_2012

Clinical questions:
- Fatigue, cold intolerance, constipation, weight gain, depression, menorrhagia.
- Amiodarone, lithium, immune checkpoint inhibitor, pregnancy/fertility context.

Focused physical exam:
- HR/BP, thyroid size/nodules.
- Delayed reflexes, edema, skin/hair.
- Mental status if severe.

Diagnostic workup and reference values:
- TSH and free T4.
- TPO antibody for autoimmune etiology.
- Pregnancy test when relevant; pituitary/adrenal evaluation if central pattern.

Reference ranges / diagnostic thresholds:
- TSH typical adult range about 0.4-4.0 mIU/L; use local lab, pregnancy, and illness context.
- Free T4 typical adult range about 0.8-1.8 ng/dL; use local assay.
- T3, TRAb/TSI, TPO Ab, and thyroglobulin Ab are assay-specific.

Red flags:
- Myxedema coma features: hypothermia, bradycardia, hypotension, hypoventilation, hyponatremia, altered mental status.

Results that change management:
- High TSH plus low FT4 confirms overt primary hypothyroidism.
- Low FT4 with low/normal TSH triggers central hypothyroidism/pituitary pathway.

## 13. Osteoporosis

Category: Bone and Parathyroid Disorders
Quality status: pass
Current app support: registry gap - needs validated clinical intent/knowledge pack before app recommendations
Guidelines: ENDO_OSTEOPOROSIS_2020

Clinical questions:
- Fragility fracture, falls, height loss/back pain.
- Glucocorticoids, aromatase inhibitors, androgen deprivation, menopause, calcium/vitamin D intake.

Focused physical exam:
- Height/weight, kyphosis/spine tenderness, gait/fall risk.
- Dental/jaw risk before antiresorptive therapy.

Diagnostic workup and reference values:
- DXA and vertebral imaging when indicated.
- Calcium, phosphorus, creatinine/eGFR, alkaline phosphatase, 25(OH)D, PTH, TSH/CBC/CMP and secondary-cause labs as indicated.

Reference ranges / diagnostic thresholds:
- Corrected calcium often about 8.5-10.5 mg/dL; ionized calcium about 1.12-1.32 mmol/L; use local lab.
- Intact PTH often about 10-65 pg/mL but assay-specific.
- DXA T-score: normal >= -1.0; osteopenia -1.0 to -2.5; osteoporosis <= -2.5.
- 25(OH)D thresholds vary; many labs flag <20 ng/mL as low and <12 ng/mL as severe deficiency, while 2024 Endocrine Society guidance avoids universal screening/target thresholds for healthy adults.

Red flags:
- New severe back pain, neurologic symptoms, hip fracture, multiple fragility fractures.

Results that change management:
- T-score <= -2.5 or fragility fracture confirms osteoporosis.
- Hip/vertebral fracture or very low T-score changes to high/very-high-risk pathway.

## 17. Vitamin D Deficiency / Osteomalacia
