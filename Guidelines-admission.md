# Admission / HPI Documentation Standard

## Purpose

Use this standard to write History and Physical reports. Tell a clear clinical story, summarize available data, support a differential diagnosis, and keep chart-ready text concise and functional. The reader must find relevant data fast.

## Rule of Separation

Place information in its designated section. Never repeat data across sections. Maintain strict boundaries between subjective reports, objective data, clinical reasoning, and action steps.

## The Full Admission Report

### Identifying Information and Chief Complaint

* Write a single opening sentence.
* State the patient age, sex, chief complaint, reason for admission, and active diagnoses relevant to the admission.

### History of Present Illness

* Write the symptom story in chronological order.
* Define time points relative to the admission time.
* Characterize the main presenting symptoms using standard acronyms.
* Document patient beliefs regarding symptom causes, exacerbating factors, and relieving factors.
* List pertinent positive and negative review of systems responses.
* Summarize prior ED visits and outpatient evaluations related to the presentation.
* Summarize the current ED course.
* Exclude random facts.

### Past Medical History

* List active medical problems and add clinical detail rather than imported EHR text.
* Include cancer stage/metastases/remission, prior coronary interventions, recent A1c and diabetic complications, HIV CD4/viral load, CKD stage/baseline creatinine, COPD stage/home oxygen, and heart-failure classification/ejection fraction when relevant.

### Medications and Allergies

* List generic name, dose, route, and frequency for each medication.
* Confirm adherence, OTC/herbal products, and the specific reaction for every allergy.
* Remove imported EHR text.

### Social History

* Write a comprehensive paragraph covering living situation, support system, current/former occupation, substance use, and functional baseline.

### Physical Exam

* Record vital signs first.
* Write a room-entry general appearance statement.
* Document pertinent positive and negative findings relative to the presentation.
* Exclude normal findings unrelated to the clinical decision.

### Data

* Summarize important laboratory and imaging results without editorial interpretation.
* Save all interpretation for Assessment.

### Assessment

* Briefly recap age, sex, the 3–4 most relevant PMH, and reason for admission in one concise sentence.
* State the clinical trajectory and formulate a new assessment distinct from prior notes.
* Explain clinical reasoning from new objective data in at most 1–2 sentences.
* Exclude subjective complaints, objective-data repetition, and action steps.

### Plan

* Reprioritize acute, chronic, and preventative problems.
* For each acute problem, state a concise differential and explain the leading diagnosis.
* Note consultant recommendations, specific daily interventions, treatments, diagnostics, and a one-sentence disposition strategy when relevant.
* Exclude clinical reasoning, patient history, and test-result repetition.
